That Feeling

There it is again
That feeling of loneliness-
cold and unbearable,
like a river on a frozen day.
It has attached itself yet again,
like a cancer spreading through my body,
taking over and slowly killing me,
drowning me as I take my last breath,
all alone-
like always
Helene

The Bird

You enter my life like a bird in migration
As darkness and coldness approach
You leave your loving impression,
Ignoring the weight of sadness
As if nothing has happened
Leaving me cold and empty.
Tearing my heart like its nothing
I shall sleep until the sun returns,
Waiting and hoping for the bird to come again
Helene

See Me

Do you even see me?
I give you all of my love,
Even my soul, I'd let you take it
For I have no need of it.

I place it before you,
A plate filled with pain and sorrow.
I give it all to you
Without regret.

And still, I go on living
Hollow, empty,
A ghost searching for a love
That was never there
Helene

Sick

This sickness is eating me alive
The sadness moves through my veins,
I can feel it as it takes control,
leaving me unlovable.
It's slowly draining my life.

As I watch the happy people,
I know it will never be me.
I am the stranger among them,
the one who can't love,
knowing I'll never be one of them.
I slowly fade away
back into the darkness
where I belong
Helene

Addiction

You have ruined everything
You just took, took, took,
Never once caring about the
Consequences.
The people we don't know anymore
Still here but with different faces,
Same voice but a different tone.
You take life,
In return you give us a ghost,
A shell of themselves,
Not wanting to return
To see a new beginning.
But you just kill, kill, kill,
Leaving nothing but pain.
Helene

Thank You

Thank you for letting me fall
into the dark hole that is life
leaving me there, inside my head,
to think of what I've done.
Darkness and guilt crush me
as the light sneaks away like
a thief in the night,
Slowly fading as it swallows me.
My throat burns as I scream for help,
nobody hears, because nobody's there.
So I lie there in my own despair
until the very last breath
Helene
